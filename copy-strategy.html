Copy Strategy · OTO Gomes

Voice rules, headline patterns, framing patterns, cross-asset style notes. Prepared 2026-05-12 by AG1 Consulting.

The brief for OTO's copy is a delicate balance: operator-credible enough to carry the tax + crypto + trust moat to a high-earning B2B-adjacent audience, and warm enough to keep the abundance / consciousness / freedom register that his existing 104K IG audience expects. The split is roughly 70% niche-leader / 30% conscious-operator. Lead with the moat, soften with the secondary register.

Voice register

Primary mode: niche-leader operator-direct, with a conscious-operator abundance overlay.

OTO is not the finance-bro register. He is not the pure-wellness register. He is the rare overlap, a credentialed tax + crypto operator with a conscious / abundance secondary layer. The copy reads accordingly:

  • Direct on the moat. "16 years tax accounting + 14 years crypto + private trust structuring" is stated factually, not bragged about.
  • Soft on the secondary register. Abundance, freedom, sovereignty, healing your money story, gamification of abundance, are deployed as secondary tokens, not the lead.
  • Anti-finance-bro. No "alpha," no "10x your portfolio," no "moon," no "diamond hands," no "WAGMI."
  • Anti-pure-wellness. No "energetic alignment with abundance," no "money is energy," no "your manifestation will return the gains."

Voice tokens to USE: - tax-aware structuring, private trust, common law, structural, the rare operator overlap - the gamification of abundance, sovereignty, freedom, abundance, healing your money story - operator, crypto cycle, the early-cycle work, holdings, position sizing, risk of total loss - teach how it actually works, the math under the mindset, the structure under the story - gateway, the bridge between, the rare combination of - educational, not personalized advice (compliance pair-language)

Voice tokens to AVOID: - moon, lambo, alpha, WAGMI, diamond hands, HODL, paper hands, ape in, NGMI, sers, gm, financial freedom (generic), passive income, side hustle, side gig, hustle, grind, boss, empire, manifest (used as financial-claim verb), unlock your potential, transform your life, harness the power, seamlessly, effortlessly, level up, paradigm shift, in today's fast-paced world, navigate the complexities, delve into, leverage synergies

Brand pillars (the through-line in every piece of copy)

  1. The rare operator overlap. 16 years tax accounting + 14 years crypto + active education on private trust + common law structuring. No competitor at his audience size can credibly claim all three.
  2. Education, not advice. OTO teaches the structures. Learners consult their own licensed professionals for personalized application. Compliance-paired language is part of the brand voice, not a footnote.
  3. The math under the mindset. Abundance + freedom + healing your money story is real, but the copy always anchors mindset claims in structural mechanism (a specific tax structure, a specific trust mechanism, a specific position-sizing rule).
  4. Sovereignty as the long-game. Crypto + trust + tax-aware structuring is framed as a wealth-sovereignty play, not a get-rich-quick play. The $20K to $17M arc is referenced as the founder story, not as the typical buyer outcome.

Framing for the funnel

  • Top of funnel (IG hooks, lead magnet, X / LinkedIn opening lines): moat-led, claim-and-mechanism, anti-hype. "The 7 tax structures crypto holders get wrong" beats "make millions in crypto" by every metric that matters at this audience tier.
  • Mid funnel (landing page, lead magnet, Circle opt-in): the tax + crypto + trust moat + the conscious-operator overlay. Specific structures, not aspiration. Founder arc as the proof anchor, not the lead.
  • High funnel (1:1, MasterHeart): for the high-earning holder who has consumed the moat content and the abundance content, knows the founder arc, and is ready for the niche-leader 1:1. Sold by reference + application gate, not by funnel.

Banned phrases (universal AG1 ban list, plus OTO-specific)

Universal AG1 ban list applies. Plus crypto-niche-specific:

  • No "guaranteed returns" / "guaranteed gains" anywhere (FTC + SEC scope)
  • No "passive income" generics (crypto holdings are not passive; tax-aware structuring is operational work)
  • No "financial freedom" generics (overused in the niche to noise)
  • No "build your crypto empire" framing (off-register for the conscious-operator brand)
  • No specific dollar-figure outcome claims in cold copy without paired FTC disclaimer ("results are not typical")
  • No "tax avoidance" framing (legal grey zone; OTO teaches legal tax structuring, not avoidance)
  • No "I'll do your taxes" framing (OTO is an educator, not a registered tax advisor; the compliance footer is non-negotiable)
  • No "secret tax loophole" framing (regulatory red flag)
  • No "100 percent done for you" on the 1:1 tier (it's a coaching engagement, not a fund management engagement)
  • No em-dash anywhere (universal AG1 rule)

Compliance pair-language (mandatory for any income-claim copy)

Standard footer for landing pages, ads, lead magnets, and any surface that mentions revenue or portfolio outcomes:

Outcomes depend on individual qualification, market conditions, position sizing, regulatory environment, and execution. OTO Gomes is an educator and is not a registered tax advisor or financial advisor. Crypto trading involves risk of substantial or total loss. Past performance is not indicative of future results. Educational content only, not personalized tax or financial advice. Consult a licensed tax professional or financial advisor before structuring any holdings. Results are not typical.

Compact inline version (single line, for ad copy or IG carousel footer slides):

Educational only · not personalized tax or financial advice · crypto involves risk of loss · past performance not indicative

This pairs with every "$20K to $17M," "$500 to $10K," "$5K-$10K mentorship" claim. The hub is AG1-internal so the inline version is fine; the public landing carries the full FTC-safe paragraph. Both versions live in landing-page.html.

Cross-asset style notes

  • Em-dash banned everywhere (universal AG1 rule). Sign-offs use ASCII hyphen "- Andrei" only.
  • The moat (16-yr tax + 14-yr crypto + trust structuring) is the first thing every cold-conversion surface mentions about OTO.
  • The founder arc ($20K to $17M, 2008 crash, 2016 divorce, 2017 fire, California acting pivot) is the second thing, as proof.
  • The conscious-operator overlay (abundance, freedom, sovereignty, healing your money story) is the third layer, as register, not as positioning.
  • Compliance pair-language is paired with every income-claim, not relegated to a footer that the cold reader doesn't see.
  • Brand colours: deep navy #011748 primary, electric green #00d084 accent, purple #9b51e0 to #7c2ad3 gradient for premium-tier surfaces, bright blue #2673ed for secondary accents.
  • The "Crypto Freedom Academy" umbrella name gets retired in favour of consistent "OTO Gomes" + tier-name conventions ("Crypto Made Easy," "Crypto Freedom Circle," "Private Mentorship"). Single founder identity, three tier names underneath.
